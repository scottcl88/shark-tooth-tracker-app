Tips for Finding Shark Teeth

Search in Shallow Waters

Shark teeth often wash up in shallow waters, especially along the shoreline where waves break. Look in ankle to knee-deep water, scanning the sand as waves recede.

Look During Low Tide

Low tide exposes more beach area, making it easier to spot shark teeth that may be hidden underwater during high tide. Plan your search during low tide for better visibility.

Pay Attention to Patterns

Shark teeth may accumulate in certain areas due to ocean currents or geological features. Look for patterns such as sandbars, ridges, or rocky outcrops, as these may concentrate shark teeth.

Search After Storms

Storms can churn up the ocean floor, exposing buried treasures like shark teeth. After a storm, visit the beach to search for freshly uncovered specimens washed ashore by rough waves.

Look Near Vegetation

Vegetation along the shoreline can trap debris, including shark teeth, as it washes ashore. Check areas near dune grass, seaweed patches, or driftwood for hidden treasures.

Watch for Different Colors

Shark teeth come in various colors, ranging from black and gray to shades of brown and even white. Train your eye to spot these colors against the sandy beach background to increase your chances of finding them.

Ask Locals for Tips

Local beachcombers and divers often have valuable insights on where to find shark teeth in the area. Don't hesitate to ask for tips and advice from those familiar with the beach.
